Details

Is it enough to be just a good programmer? Or are you more than that? In this contest you will have to write your own code, along with it you have to understand the code of your team-mate because you will switch places with tour partner after fixed intervals. So let's see how can you solve the problem, without any actual communication!

Rules

  • The robot must be able to fit inside a box with dimensions of 25 cm X 25 cm X 25 cm (l x b x h) and weigh less than 3 kg.
  • Robot behaviour must be non-offensive, non-destructive, and non-harmful to humans and the facilities. This is an immutable principle, even if the behaviour is unintentional or not by design.
  • The machine must use only mechanical power or mechanical power converted from a source of electrical energy.
  • The machine must be fully autonomous. i.e., no manual control is to be present.
  • During inspection (and at any time during the event), the judges may require safety changes or other modifications to meet the harmlessness requirement. Harmful robots are either not allowed to compete at all or are later disqualified if potential harmful issues are proven or revealed in battle.
  • The machine should not leave spare parts in the arena at any stage. This can lead to disqualification or may even lead to round loss.


  • All the students enrolled in high school, undergraduate, postgraduate (excluding PhD.) program at any Recognized institute (identity card will be checked) are eligible to participate.
  • A team can consist of a maximum of 4 members.
  • Each team must declare a name for their machine at the time of competition.


Round 1

  • The Robot Sumo ring is a large, flat circular arena (Dohyo) of diameter 50cm.
  • The Robot Sumo ring is a large, flat circular arena (Dohyo) of diameter 50cm.
  • The ring is raised slightly to make it easier to determine when a robot has been pushed out. The height is raised such that it doesn’t damage robots that fall or get pushed out.
  • An external area of at least 100 centimeters (3.3 feet) of empty space exists around the ring. This space must not contain any people, objects, lights, or anything else that would distract or interfere with the robots.

Final Round

  • The arena is in the form of ellipse of maximum length 200 cm and maximum width of 100 cm.
  • The top surface is dull black, except for a thin border of 5cm width that is shiny white.
  • The ring is raised slightly to make it easier to determine when a robot has been pushed out. The height is raised such that it doesn’t damage robots that fall or get pushed out.
  • An external area of at least 100 centimeters (3.3 feet) of empty space exists around the ring. This space must not contain any people, objects, lights, or anything else that would distract or interfere with the robots.


Round 1

  • The 2 fighting bots will be kept at the same distance from each other and from the edge, just before the round begins.
  • One round will be of 3 minutes.
  • There will be total of 3 rounds.
  • To win the round the bot must push or throw the opponent out of the arena completely.

Final Round

  • The 2 bots will be placed at different ends of the arena not facing each other, just before the round begins.
  • One round will be of 4 minutes.
  • There will be total of 3 rounds.
  • To win the round the bot must push or throw the opponent out of the arena completely
